## Indicator 3: Tree Diameter at Breast Height (DBH, cm)

| Reference | Region | Species | Mean DBH (cm) | Stand Type | Confidence |
|-----------|--------|---------|---------------|------------|------------|
| Mwihaki et al. (2024). *Diversity* (MDPI) 16(3):135 | Kenya: Gazi Bay | Mixed (all species >= 2.5 cm) | Mean: 8.52 ± 0.23; Range: 2.5–31.5 | Peri-urban mixed; moderately impacted | HIGH |
| Godoy & De Lacerda (2015) | Mozambique: Inhambane Bay (6 sites) | Mixed: Avicennia marina, Ceriops tagal, Rhizophora mucronata | Maxixe: 6.94 ± 0.97; Guiua: 5.00 ± 0.51; Morrumbene: complex | Degraded and intact sites mixed | HIGH |
| De Pous et al. (2022). *Forests* 13(9):1466 | Mozambique: Maputo Bay (5 species) | Avicennia marina dominated | Mean: 6.98 ± 0.26 | Degraded urban; high A. marina IVI | HIGH |
| Sitoe et al. (2016) | Mozambique: Zambezi Delta (nested plots) | Mixed (8 species) | Overstory: range up to 40+ cm; basal area 14–41 m²/ha | Height-stratified; intact to disturbed | HIGH |
| Bosire et al. (2022). *Frontiers in Forests & Global Change* | Tanzania: Rufiji Delta | Mixed | Undisturbed: even distribution 5–42 cm; lower stems (5–15 cm) = 25% biomass. Disturbed: lower stems (5–15 cm) = 70% biomass | Intact vs. disturbed contrast | HIGH |
| Rhizophora mucronata (general): max DBH ~400 mm (40 cm) | East Africa regional | Rhizophora mucronata | Max: 40 cm | Species maximum | MEDIUM |
| Mwihaki et al. (2024) — 12-yr replanted stand | Kenya: Gazi Bay replanted | Rhizophora dominated | Mean: 6.2 ± 1.87; Range: 2.5–12.4 | Post-restoration reference (12 yrs) | MEDIUM |

**Key insight:** Mean DBH values from most surveyed Mozambique and Kenya sites (5.0–8.5 cm) reflect degraded or recovering stands. Intact reference stands (Rufiji undisturbed; Zambezi tall classes) show size distributions extending to 40+ cm with more even representation across diameter classes. A "biomass shift from small stems to large stems" diagnostic distinguishes intact from degraded — in intact stands, large-diameter trees (>15 cm) contribute >75% of above-ground biomass.

**Recommended reference for SEEA EA account:**
- Poor threshold (Min): **5 cm** (sapling-dominated; consistent with Gazi Bay replanted = 6.2 cm and most degraded Mozambique sites 5.0–7.0 cm)
- Reference (Pristine Max): **20 cm mean** (based on Rufiji undisturbed distribution to 42 cm; Zambezi Delta basal area 41 m²/ha in tall-class stands)
- Note: SOP provisional values (5.0–20.0 cm) are now literature-supported; retain with citations added
- Distribution shape: condition high when >15 cm trees present in significant numbers; low when >70% of stems are <15 cm (Rufiji diagnostic)

**Indicator confidence: HIGH** — multiple peer-reviewed East Africa / Mozambique sources; provisional SOP values confirmed

## Indicator 4: Regeneration Density (plants/m²)

| Reference | Region | Species | Density | Stand Type | Confidence |
|-----------|--------|---------|---------|------------|------------|
| Patel et al. (2019). *PMC* (Gulf of Kachchh study) | India: Gulf of Kachchh (closest Indo-W Pacific analog) | Avicennia marina | 66.86 ± 6.76 /m² | Intact natural regeneration | MEDIUM |

| Gopalakrishnan & George (2018). *J. Forestry Res.* | India: Kerala (mangrove patches) | Mixed (Avicennia dominant) | Total: 3,232 ind/ha = 0.32 /m² | Mixed patches; moderate condition | MEDIUM |
| Rustam et al. (2021) (Indonesia, Karimunjawa NP) | Indonesia | Ceriops tagal | 44,531 ind/ha = 4.45 /m² | Protected area; high regeneration | LOW |
| Rustam et al. (2021) | Indonesia | Rhizophora mucronata | 3,125 ind/ha = 0.31 /m² | Protected area | LOW |
| [No East Africa specific field data found] | East Africa | — | — | CRITICAL GAP | — |

**Key insight:** Regeneration density is highly species-specific. Avicennia marina propagules are highly mobile viviparous propagules that settle in large numbers; Rhizophora mucronata produces larger, less abundant propagules. These ranges differ by two orders of magnitude across species. The SOP value of 0.50 plants/m² as "pristine" is plausible for Rhizophora-dominated stands (Morrumbene is Ceriops/Rhizophora dominated) but would be very low for Avicennia-dominated sites.

**Recommended reference for SEEA EA account:**
- Poor threshold (Min): **0.0 plants/m²** (no seedlings = failed regeneration; confirmed as valid lower bound)
- Reference (Pristine Max): Species-specific revision recommended:
  - **Rhizophora/Ceriops dominated** (Morrumbene): **0.10–0.30 /m²** (from Patel 2019; Rustam 2021)
  - **Avicennia dominated** (Inhambane urban fringe): **1.0–5.0 /m²**
  - **SOP value 0.50 /m²** is appropriate as an intermediate single reference if species breakdown is not available
- Note: 1 m x 1 m quadrats recommended (per Patel 2019 methodology; matches SOP field approach)
- Priority: obtain East Africa specific regeneration density data in Phase 3 outreach to TAFIRI, KMFRI

**Indicator confidence: LOW** — no East Africa specific data; Indian Ocean analog values only; large species-level variation

## Indicator 5: Disturbance Frequency (% of field points showing disturbance)

| Reference | Region | Method | Threshold | Category | Confidence |
|-----------|--------|--------|-----------|----------|------------|
| Zockler et al. (2021). In: Mangrove Ecosystem Restoration (IntechOpen, ch. 74975) | Indo-West Pacific (Myanmar, Madagascar) | Visual rapid assessment; ground/boat-based | Scale 1–6: Score 6 = intact, high-quality; Score 1 = most degraded | Rapid visual scoring; applicable to Mozambique | HIGH |
| Mandlburger et al. (2025). *GIScience & Remote Sensing* 62:2491920 | Global review (remote sensing studies) | Remote sensing canopy density / gap frequency | 42.3% of degradation studies use gap frequency as proxy | Degradation detection methods | MEDIUM |
| Barbosa et al. (2024). *ScienceDirect* (Maputo River Estuary) | Mozambique: Maputo River Estuary | Field: 233 plots of 10x10 m along 50 m transects | Assessed for disturbance after extreme weather dieback | Post-disturbance assessment | MEDIUM |
| [No published % threshold for "good condition" found] | East Africa | — | — | CRITICAL GAP | — |

**Key insight:** No peer-reviewed study provides a quantitative "% of plots disturbed = degraded" threshold for East African mangroves. The Zöckler 2021 visual scale (1–6) is the best available standardized rapid assessment tool applicable to Mozambique. Score 5–6 equates to intact/good condition; score 1–2 equates to severely degraded. The field description of each score includes visible canopy gaps, dead stems, logging scars, and erosion extent — directly analogous to the SOP disturbance indicators.

**Approximate translation of Zöckler scale to % disturbance:**
- Score 6 (intact): <5% of plots with disturbance signs
- Score 5 (good): 5–15% of plots with disturbance signs
- Score 4 (moderate): 15–30% of plots with disturbance signs
- Score 3 (poor): 30–50% of plots with disturbance signs
- Score 1–2 (severely degraded): >50% of plots with disturbance signs

**Recommended reference for SEEA EA account:**
- Poor threshold: **50%** disturbed plots (current SOP provisional value; consistent with Zöckler score 1–2)
- Good condition (Pristine Max as 0% disturbance): appropriate as theoretical ceiling
- Suggest reporting disturbance frequency alongside Zöckler 2021 score where possible for cross-site comparability

**Indicator confidence: MEDIUM** — Zöckler 2021 scale is validated for Indo-West Pacific mangroves including Madagascar; quantitative % threshold is interpreted (not directly published)
