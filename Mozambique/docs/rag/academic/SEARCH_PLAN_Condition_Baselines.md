# Search Plan: Finding Baseline Reference Levels for Condition Indicators
**Mozambique Mangrove MFT1.2 Condition Account**

---

## Overview

This document provides a systematic search strategy to locate **baseline/reference values** for each ecosystem condition indicator from peer-reviewed literature, with regional focus on **Western Indian Ocean (WIO), East African, and global tropical mangrove ecosystems**.

**Goal:** Validate and refine the provisional reference levels currently in the SOP (`skill_extent_mangrove_m13_mozambique.md`, Section 4.1); identify regionally specific values where available.

**Condition indicators covered:**
1. Canopy density (% cover, 0–1 scale)
2. Canopy height (m)
3. Tree diameter at breast height (DBH, cm)
4. Regeneration density (saplings/plants per m²)
5. Disturbance frequency (% of field points disturbed)

## INDICATOR 1: CANOPY DENSITY (% cover or 0–1 scale)

### Current Reference
- **Provisional SOP values:** Poor = 0.30; Pristine = 0.70 (revised downward from 0.95 on 2026-03-29)
- **Issue:** No literature source cited; values are expert estimates; no WIO or East African empirical baseline documented

### What Counts as a Valid Baseline
- Peer-reviewed studies reporting mangrove canopy cover or closure (%, fraction 0–1, or LAI)
- Explicit reference condition: intact, undisturbed, old-growth, or protected mangrove stand
- Method transparency: hemispherical photography, densiometer, or LiDAR-derived canopy cover
- Regional context: WIO, East Africa, or tropical mangrove (global reference acceptable if method clear)
- Multi-site averages (n >= 3 plots) preferred over single-site observations

AND ("East Africa" OR "Western Indian Ocean" OR "WIO" OR "Mozambique" OR "Tanzania"

**Variant searches:**

- Reference condition: old-growth, primary, undisturbed, or highly protected stands

## INDICATOR 3: TREE DIAMETER AT BREAST HEIGHT (DBH, cm)

### Current Reference
- **Provisional SOP values:** Poor = 5.0 cm; Pristine = 20.0 cm (from Section 4.1)
- **Issue:** DBH values are highly species and age-class dependent; no WIO regional reference cited; older (larger-diameter) trees indicate greater ecosystem integrity

### What Counts as a Valid Baseline
- Peer-reviewed studies reporting mean or median DBH (cm) at 1.3 m height for mangrove species
- Clear reference condition: old-growth, primary, protected, or "reference" stand
- Species-specific or mixed-stand DBH distributions from East Africa or WIO
- Sample size: n >= 10 trees per species/site; plot-level means preferred
- Regional context: WIO, East Africa, or comparable tropical mangrove latitude

### Search Terms & Modifiers
**Primary searches:**
```
("mangrove" OR "mangrove forest") AND ("DBH" OR "diameter at breast height" OR "stem diameter"
OR "basal area" OR "diameter distribution")
AND ("reference" OR "undisturbed" OR "intact" OR "old-growth" OR "pristine")
AND ("East Africa" OR "Western Indian Ocean" OR "WIO" OR "Mozambique" OR "Tanzania"
     OR "Kenya" OR "Indian Ocean")
```

**Variant searches:**
- `("Rhizophora" OR "Avicennia" OR "Sonneratia" OR "Ceriops" OR "Bruguiera") AND ("DBH" OR "diameter") AND ("Africa" OR "Indian Ocean")`

## INDICATOR 4: REGENERATION DENSITY (saplings/plants per m²)

### Current Reference
- **Provisional SOP values:** Poor = 0.0 plants/m²; Pristine = 0.50 plants/m² (from Section 4.1)
- **Issue:** No WIO or East African reference cited; regeneration is highly context-dependent (species, season, disturbance history); propagule density varies enormously

### What Counts as a Valid Baseline
- Peer-reviewed studies reporting mangrove seedling or sapling density (ind/m², ind/ha, or count/plot)
- Explicit reference condition: actively regenerating intact stand, healthy fringe mangrove, or post-disturbance recovery
- Species-level or total regeneration counts from WIO or East African sites
- Sampling method clear: quadrat size, height threshold for seedling/sapling definition
- Regional context: WIO, East Africa, or tropical mangrove globally

### Search Terms & Modifiers
**Primary searches:**
```
("mangrove" OR "mangrove forest") AND ("regeneration" OR "recruitment" OR "seedling density"

AND ("East Africa" OR "Western Indian Ocean" OR "WIO" OR "Mozambique" OR "Tanzania"
     OR "Kenya" OR "Indian Ocean" OR "tropical")
AND ("reference" OR "intact" OR "healthy" OR "natural regeneration" OR "undisturbed")

### Current Reference
- **Provisional SOP values:** High disturbance = 50% (scored as 0); Undisturbed = 0% (scored as 1)

### What Counts as a Valid Baseline
- Peer-reviewed studies reporting mangrove disturbance prevalence, cover of damage, or degradation indices
- Quantitative metrics: % stems with logging scars, % degraded area, erosion indicators per plot

- Comparable field methods: point-intercept or visual disturbance scoring
- Regional context: WIO, East Africa, or tropical mangrove globally

## CROSS-CUTTING SEARCHES: Regional Mangrove Condition Baselines

### Search Strategy: "WIO Mangrove Condition & Structure"
For indicators where regional WIO data is sparse, conduct broader ecosystem structural searches:

**Search terms:**
```
("mangrove forest" OR "mangrove ecosystem") AND ("condition" OR "health" OR "structural integrity"
OR "ecosystem state")
AND ("Western Indian Ocean" OR "WIO" OR "East Africa" OR "Mozambique" OR "Tanzania" OR "Kenya")
AND ("canopy" OR "height" OR "DBH" OR "regeneration" OR "biomass")
AND (year >= 2010)
AND ("intact" OR "degraded" OR "reference" OR "baseline")
```

**Key geographic targets:**
- **Tanzania** — Best-studied East African mangroves; Rufiji delta has extensive structural data
- **Kenya** — Gazi Bay long-term monitoring; KMFRI publications; canopy + biomass references
- **Madagascar** — Some WIO mangrove condition data (secondary)
- **Mozambique** — Growing literature; Sofala, Zambezi delta; Save Estuary specific data

### Search Strategy: "Global Mangrove Reference Baselines"
Use global mangrove databases and atlases as secondary references where WIO data is absent:

**Search terms:**
```
("mangrove" OR "mangrove forest") AND ("global" OR "pantropical" OR "tropical mangrove")
AND ("canopy height" OR "DBH" OR "basal area" OR "above-ground biomass")
AND ("reference" OR "baseline" OR "intact" OR "undisturbed")
AND (Spalding OR Giri OR Hamilton OR Worthington OR Bunting)
```

**Key references to retrieve:**
- Spalding et al. 2010 — World Atlas of Mangroves (structural data by region)

**Search terms:**

("mangrove" OR "mangrove forest") AND ("marine protected area" OR "MPA" OR "protected area"
OR "conservation area" OR "community conservation")
AND ("canopy" OR "height" OR "DBH" OR "regeneration" OR "biomass")
AND ("inside-outside" OR "protected vs unprotected" OR "before-after")
AND ("Africa" OR "Indian Ocean" OR "WIO")
```

## CRITICAL APPRAISAL CHECKLIST

When evaluating a source for inclusion as a "baseline":

- [ ] **Peer-reviewed or credible institution?** (>= MEDIUM confidence)
- [ ] **Methods transparent?** (Can I assess comparability to SOP field methods — 25 m resolution, visual scoring?)
- [ ] **Sample size adequate?** (n >= 3 plots for multi-site averages; or clear single-site context)
- [ ] **Reference condition explicit?** (Does the paper state "intact," "old-growth," "undisturbed," or "protected"?)
- [ ] **Regional relevance?** (WIO, East Africa, or clearly analogous tropical mangrove)
- [ ] **Temporal specificity?** (When was data collected? Recent <10 yrs preferred for condition; structural allometrics can be older)
- [ ] **Quantitative values reported?** (Actual numbers with units, not qualitative descriptions)
- [ ] **Uncertainty ranges?** (SD, SE, CI, or range provided?)
- [ ] **Species context?** (Key Mozambique species: Rhizophora mucronata, Avicennia marina, Sonneratia alba, Ceriops tagal, Bruguiera gymnorrhiza)

## EXECUTION ROADMAP

### Phase 1: Quick Scan (1 week)
1. Google Scholar quick search for each indicator (5–10 min per indicator)
2. Identify 5–10 promising papers per indicator
3. Extract title, authors, year, region, key values
4. Priority targets: Tanzanian Rufiji delta studies; Kenya Gazi Bay; Spalding 2010 Atlas

### Phase 2: Detailed Review (2 weeks)
1. Access full-text papers (institutional library, ResearchGate, or contact authors)
2. Document reference values in standardised table (see outputs below)
3. Assess comparability to SOP field methods (visual scoring, 25 m resolution)
4. Flag data gaps (e.g., "no WIO data found for regeneration density")

### Phase 3: Regional Outreach (1 week)
1. Email WIO mangrove researchers (ResearchGate contacts: TAFIRI, KMFRI, AQUA-CSP, Eduardo Mondlane University)
2. Query MICOA (Mozambique Ministry of Land & Environment), WWF Mozambique, WCS for grey literature
3. Check FAO, IUCN, Wetlands International, UNEP-WCMC mangrove assessment reports
4. Request GMW team for any associated structural data tables

### Phase 4: Synthesis (1 week)
1. Consolidate findings in standardised reference table
2. Identify gaps (e.g., "no WIO regeneration baseline found; use global tropical mangrove reference")
3. Revise SOP Section 4.1 reference values where literature-supported values differ from provisional
4. Write short methods note: "Reference level validation and regional comparison" section for publication
