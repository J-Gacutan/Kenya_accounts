About

Partners and Acknowledgements

Pwani University

Pwani University leads the field data collection and ecological monitoring that underpins Kenya's coastal ecosystem accounts. Under the supervision of Professor Bernerd Fulanda, Dr Juliet Karisa, and Dr Pascal Thoya, Pwani University research teams coordinated multi-site surveys across coral reefs, mangroves, seagrass meadows, and fisheries landing sites along Kilifi County's coastline between 2024 and 2025. This work encompassed underwater visual census (UVC) transects across 53 reef sites through three survey programmes (CCVA/KMFRI and PU-GOAP in Nov 2024; COMRED Oceans in Jul 2025), structural plot surveys across 32 mangrove plots in 4 areas, 3,040 seagrass quadrat assessments at 9 survey areas, and collation of 5 years of Kenya Fisheries Service landing records. Pwani University's field expertise and institutional relationships with KMFRI, the Kenya Wildlife Service, and coastal community organisations are central to sustaining the monitoring infrastructure needed for regular account compilation.

Global Ocean Accounts Partnership (GOAP)

The Global Ocean Accounts Partnership is a multi-stakeholder initiative that supports countries to measure and manage progress towards ocean sustainable development through standardised ecosystem accounting. GOAP is an endorsed UN Ocean Decade Action, co-chaired by the Government of Indonesia and The Charles Darwin Foundation, with a target of supporting at least 30 countries by 2030 to build national ocean accounts aligned with the UN System of Environmental-Economic Accounting (SEEA). GOAP's current phase of work (Phase 3, funded by the UK Blue Planet Fund) includes regional expansion across East Africa, the Pacific, and the Caribbean, alongside technical guidance development, capacity building, and integration of ocean accounts into national policy frameworks. Kenya's Kilifi County ecosystem accounts are part of GOAP's East Africa programme, contributing to a growing body of pilot accounts across the Western Indian Ocean region.

UNSW Centre for Sustainable Development Reform (CSDR)

The Centre for Sustainable Development Reform at the University of New South Wales (UNSW Sydney) hosts the GOAP Secretariat and provides the technical and methodological backbone for ocean accounting programmes worldwide. Under the direction of Dr Ben Milligan, CSDR's team of over 25 researchers and practitioners develops natural capital accounting frameworks, conducts country-level pilot accounts, builds regional communities of practice, and translates ecosystem science into policy-ready outputs. CSDR's expertise spans SEEA ecosystem accounting, marine spatial planning, blue carbon finance, remote sensing, environmental valuation, and ocean governance. The Kenya pilot draws on CSDR's experience delivering national and sub-national ecosystem accounts in Indonesia, the Maldives, Fiji, the Solomon Islands, and other partner countries, applying lessons learned from over a decade of applied ocean accounting research.
