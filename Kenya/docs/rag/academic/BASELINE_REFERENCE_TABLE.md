## Theme 5: Blue Carbon Revenue Projection

| Reference | Metric | Value | Assumptions | Confidence |
|-----------|--------|-------|-------------|------------|
| Derived from sources _006 + _007 | Annual mangrove carbon revenue (Lamu + Kwale combined) | ~US$3.59 million/year | Carbon price: US$20/tCO₂e; full area credited; annual sequestration not annual stock | LOW |

**Derivation notes:**
- Revenue = annual sequestration rate (Mg CO₂e/ha/yr) x area (ha) x carbon price (US$/tCO₂e)
- The 560.23 and 526.34 values may represent total stock (Mg C ha⁻¹), not annual sequestration; the conversion requires a separate annual uptake rate (typically 0.5–1.5 Mg C ha⁻¹/yr for healthy mangroves)
- If values represent total stock, the revenue calculation may conflate stock with flow — a critical methodological distinction for SEEA EA accounts
- Carbon price sensitivity: US$10/tCO₂e gives ~US$1.8M/year; US$50/tCO₂e gives ~US$9M/year

**Recommended reference for SEEA EA account:**
- Report projected revenue as a range: US$1.8M–US$9M/year depending on carbon price (US$10–US$50/tCO₂e)
- Clarify whether density values are stock or flow; seek original Go Blue project methodology
- Compare to Vanga Blue Forest (Kwale) actual credit issuance volumes when available
- Do NOT use US$3.59M as a single point estimate without sensitivity range

**Indicator confidence: LOW** — modeled; sensitive to carbon price and stock-vs-flow uncertainty; area data not yet confirmed

CRITICAL NOTE — Quadrat conversion for Kilifi field data: The Kilifi dataset uses 15x15 cm quadrats. Conversion to shoots/m²: multiply count by 44.4 (= 1 m² / 0.0225 m²). ### S3 — Species Composition and Assemblage Health

Reference assemblage for healthy Kilifi County seagrass (based on Ochieng & Erftemeijer 2003, Gullstrom et al. 2002, Uku & Bjork 2005):

| Zone | Dominant species | Condition signal |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Nearshore / intertidal fringe | Halodule uninervis, Halophila ovalis, Cymodocea rotundata | Normal pioneer zone |
| Mid-intertidal to shallow subtidal (0.5–2 m) | Cymodocea rotundata, Cymodocea serrulata, Thalassia hemprichii | Highest diversity; reference condition |
| Subtidal (2–5 m) | Thalassia hemprichii, Syringodium isoetifolium, Halophila stipulacea | Shade-tolerant / deeper-reaching species |
| Deep subtidal / rocky margin (>5 m) | Thalassodendron ciliatum | Specialist; indicates clear oligotrophic water |

Condition interpretation: 11 species recorded across 10 Kilifi sites is high diversity, consistent with a relatively intact WIO meadow system. Presence of Thalassodendron ciliatum is a positive water quality signal (Bandeira 2002; Mwangi et al. 2021, Aquatic Botany). Degradation signals: Halodule uninervis expanding at expense of Thalassia/Cymodocea; complete loss of Thalassodendron; monospecific Halodule beds (Uku & Bjork 2005).

Interpreting the +172% Kilifi catch growth (2020–2024): Three non-exclusive explanations apply, in descending probability. (1) Improved monitoring coverage — SWIOFISH2 (World Bank, 2018–2023) substantially expanded KeFS landing site coverage in coastal counties, with pre-2020 observer coverage likely below 30% (Mbaru et al. 2020). (2) COVID-19 base effect — 2020 baseline (4,452 MT) is almost certainly depressed by 30–50% activity reduction (FAO 2021, Impacts of COVID-19 on fisheries). (3) Genuine catch increase — biologically implausible at this magnitude given CPUE decline trend in underlying stock literature; if real, would indicate unsustainable exploitation given OHI B/BMSY ~0.65. Recommended approach: use 2017–2019 average (if KeFS data obtainable from Dr. Pascal Thoya) as baseline rather than 2020.
