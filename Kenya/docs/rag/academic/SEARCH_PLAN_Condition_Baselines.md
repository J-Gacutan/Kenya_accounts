# Search Plan: Finding Evidence for Kenya Blue Economy Indicators
**Kenya Blue Economy — SEEA EA Accounts**

---

## Overview

This document provides a systematic search strategy to locate **peer-reviewed evidence and primary data sources** for each key indicator in Kenya's blue economy accounts. The Phase 1 strategic document analysis identified core data points from gray literature; this search plan targets peer-reviewed corroboration, primary survey sources, and regional WIO evidence.

**Goal:** Validate and contextualize the data values currently sourced from gray literature (government strategies, project reports); identify peer-reviewed support where available; flag gaps that require primary data collection.

**Thematic areas covered:**
1. Blue carbon — mangrove carbon density (Lamu, Kwale)
2. Blue carbon — seagrass carbon density (Lamu, Kwale)
3. Blue carbon — annual sequestration rates (flow vs. stock)
4. Fisheries production and ecosystem baselines
5. IUU fishing economic costs
6. Post-harvest losses in coastal fisheries
7. Marine Spatial Planning effectiveness

## AREA 1: MANGROVE CARBON DENSITY (Mg C ha⁻¹)

### Current Reference
- **Phase 1 values:** Lamu = 560.23 Mg C ha⁻¹; Kwale = 526.34 Mg C ha⁻¹ (source: Go Blue project report; gray literature)
- **Issue:** Primary survey methodology not confirmed; stock vs. annual flow distinction unclear; no peer-reviewed WIO comparison identified

### What Counts as a Valid Source
- Peer-reviewed studies reporting total ecosystem carbon (above-ground + below-ground + soil organic carbon) for East African or WIO mangroves
- Field-based measurements (cores, allometric equations) with explicit sampling design
- Site-specific data from Lamu County or Kwale County; or Kenya Coastal region broadly
- Method transparency: core depth, bulk density, loss on ignition or elemental analysis

### Current Reference
- **Phase 1 gap:** No annual sequestration rate values identified; revenue projection may conflate stock with flow
- **Issue:** SEEA EA accounts require annual flow, not stock; without sequestration rate, carbon revenue projections are unreliable

### What Counts as a Valid Source
- Peer-reviewed studies reporting annual net primary production or carbon accumulation in mangroves or seagrass
- Eddy covariance, sediment core chronology, or biomass increment methods
- East African or WIO sites strongly preferred; global tropical analogs acceptable

### Search Terms & Modifiers
**Primary searches:**
```
("mangrove" OR "seagrass") AND ("annual" OR "yearly") AND ("carbon sequestration"
OR "carbon accumulation" OR "net primary production" OR "carbon uptake rate"
OR "Mg C ha yr" OR "tC ha yr")
AND ("East Africa" OR "WIO" OR "Kenya" OR "Indian Ocean" OR "tropical")
```

**Variant searches:**
- `("mangrove carbon sequestration rate") AND ("annual") AND ("Africa" OR "Indian Ocean")`
- `("blue carbon sequestration") AND ("WIO" OR "East Africa") AND (year >= 2012)`
- `Donato AND mangrove AND carbon AND (2011 OR 2012) AND ("stock" OR "sequestration")`

### Target Journals & Databases
| Source | Rationale |
|--------|-----------|
| **Nature Climate Change** | Donato et al. 2011 mangrove carbon storage |
| **Global Biogeochemical Cycles** | Mangrove carbon cycling; WIO studies |

### Success Criteria

## CRITICAL APPRAISAL CHECKLIST

When evaluating a source for inclusion in Kenya's RAG:

- [ ] **Peer-reviewed or credible institution?** (>= MEDIUM confidence)
- [ ] **Methods transparent?** (Can I assess comparability to SEEA EA accounting methods?)
- [ ] **Sample size adequate?** (For carbon: n >= 3 plots; for fisheries: multi-year or multi-site data)
- [ ] **Reference condition explicit?** (For carbon: are measurements from intact or degraded sites? For fisheries: is baseline year stated?)
- [ ] **Regional relevance?** (Kenya, East Africa, WIO, or clearly analogous tropical coastal ecosystem)
- [ ] **Temporal specificity?** (When was data collected? Carbon stocks and fisheries baselines change over time)
- [ ] **Quantitative values reported?** (Actual numbers with units, not qualitative descriptions)
- [ ] **Uncertainty ranges?** (SD, SE, CI, or range provided?)
- [ ] **Stock vs. flow distinction?** (Critical for SEEA EA: stock = total carbon; flow = annual sequestration)
- [ ] **Gray literature flag?** (Government reports are valid but should be flagged and corroborated where possible)
