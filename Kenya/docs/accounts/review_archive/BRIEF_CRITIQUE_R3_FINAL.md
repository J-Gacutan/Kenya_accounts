---

**Condition 5: Define the value of the "estimated pristine reef baseline" used to normalise coral cover CI.**

RESOLVED. The inline note in the coral cover CI cell now reads: "Cover normalised against an estimated pristine condition reference of approximately 50% (consistent with GCRMN good-excellent condition benchmarks for Indo-Pacific reefs), not the 30% minimum threshold; the 30% value marks the lower bound of acceptable condition only. ## SECTION 2: REVIEWER 4 ADDITIONAL CHANGES -- STATUS CHECK

The R2 critique did not present three formally numbered "Reviewer 4 additional changes." The three new issues raised by Reviewer 4 in R2 were: (a) "generally good condition" overstatement for seagrass; (b) Mida Creek "management benefit" claim; (c) 30x30/GBF Target 3 framing using a single weak comparison. These were listed as new issues, not as conditions of approval. They are checked here for implementation nonetheless.

---

**Reviewer 4 Change (a): Seagrass "generally good condition" overstatement given LOW confidence ratings.**

DONE. Page 1 now reads: "Seagrass meadows across 10 accounting areas in Kilifi County produce provisional composite condition indices in the moderate-to-high range (0.69 to 0.94), noting that all seagrass reference levels carry LOW confidence ratings and these indices should be treated as directional only." The phrase "generally good condition" has been replaced with a range statement that no longer makes a qualitative condition claim. The framing is now consistent with the confidence level.

---

**Reviewer 4 Change (b): Mida Creek "management benefit" claim -- add confounding factor acknowledgement.**

DONE. Page 1 now reads: "Mida Creek consistently produces the highest condition indices among mangrove areas, consistent with its Ramsar site designation and associated management -- though site characteristics and creek morphology may also contribute to this differential." The hedging phrase acknowledges site-characteristic confounders and removes the unqualified attribution of performance to management alone. The GEF Annex B row still references "governance performance differentials (Mida Creek vs. Kilifi Creek)" as evidence -- this is acceptable because the differential is real and observable; the key issue was the unqualified causal attribution in the narrative.

---

**Reviewer 4 Change (c): 30x30/GBF PA expansion framing using a single data point.**

DONE. The 2-5 year recommendation now reads: "A formal PA effectiveness analysis, drawing on the condition differential observed between Watamu No-Take MPA (CI 0.794) and unprotected sites (CI 0.635), should be commissioned to test whether the observed differential is attributable to protection status after controlling for site characteristics. This would provide the evidentiary basis for future PA expansion decisions." The language "commissioned to test" correctly positions the accounts as a starting hypothesis rather than confirmed evidence, and PA expansion is now framed as a downstream outcome of the analysis rather than the primary recommendation.

### SIGNIFICANT issues (note; do not block)

**Issue 1 (SEEA EA Technical / Coral Cover CI): Residual discrepancy between the stated CI formula and the reported value is disclosed but unresolved.**

The coral cover CI cell now openly states that 40/50 = 0.80 but the account reports 0.905, attributing the difference to the multi-indicator CI calculation. This is not wrong to disclose but it introduces an unexplained numerical inconsistency that a technical auditor will flag. The discrepancy is likely due to how the multi-indicator aggregation interacts with the per-indicator normalisation -- but this is not explained. The note correctly redirects to the R script, and since the R analysis is the definitive source, this is manageable. However, if this brief is used in a peer-reviewed or official SEEA EA reporting context, the aggregation arithmetic should be made consistent with the explanatory note. Flag for resolution before formal publication; acceptable for government and finance stakeholder review.

**Issue 2 (Government): The NBES KPI recommendation still lacks a standalone action-line structure.**

R2 Reviewer 1 flagged that the NBES KPI adoption recommendation (coral fish biomass target, coral recruitment rate, mangrove CI as NBES headline KPIs) is embedded in the third paragraph of the 0-6 month block without its own Responsible/Output/Timeline/Resource structure. This has not been changed in v3 -- the recommendation still reads as supporting context for the coral recruitment paragraph rather than a standalone action. For a PS-level audience this is a visibility risk. Not a blocker for stakeholder review; note for any revision prior to official government tabling.
