## R2 Condition Checks

**Condition 1: Resolve the Ngomeni integrated seascape claim (Issue 1.4)**
RESOLVED. Section 4.1A now reclassifies the Ngomeni integrated seascape profile as "[deferred to Phase 2]" with explicit explanation that fisheries data cannot be disaggregated to site level and no spatial join exists; a qualitative Ngomeni callout using per-ecosystem site data is permitted as a substitute, which is a defensible position that will not expose the brief to a data-availability challenge.

**Condition 2: Add provisional data disclosures for fisheries monetary values and blue carbon total value claims (Issues 1.7, 2.6, 2.7)**
RESOLVED. Section 4.1B now carries explicit disclosure notes for both items: fisheries monetary values are flagged as provisional pending Dr. Thoya attribution fraction validation, with the sensitivity range specified as the primary policy-facing figure in the interim; the blue carbon total value estimate is explicitly conditioned on GMW 2020 extent retrieval and restricted to per-hectare reference values until that retrieval is complete.

**Condition 3: Correct the NBES submission timeline and fix the Section 10 internal contradiction (Issues 3.3, 1.6)**
RESOLVED. Section 8 now reads "Q4 2026 to feed NBES mid-term review evidence base," correcting the Q1 2027 date that would have missed the annual reporting cycle; Section 10 Open Items now reads "OUTPUTS AVAILABLE — canonical plan sign-off pending," consistent with the Section 6.1 blocking note and the language used throughout Sections 1 and 4.1A.

## Final Panel Verdict: APPROVED

The SOW is fit for purpose. Policy brief drafting can begin.

All three R2 blocking conditions have been addressed with substantive changes, not cosmetic edits. The Ngomeni deferral is properly bounded; the provisional data flags are now embedded in the evidence base section where a DFI officer or ministry analyst will encounter them before relying on the figures; and the internal contradiction between Section 10 and Section 6.1 has been corrected. The remaining open items from R2 — PES labelling (Issue 2.5), KNBS consultation inclusion (Issue 3.5), and the minor KWS briefing timeline clarification (Issue 3.6) — are improvements that can be addressed during drafting without blocking the brief's credibility. A Principal Secretary can use the brief produced from this SOW in a budget meeting, because the governance gap analysis is concrete, the data confidence tiers are disclosed, and the recommendations are mapped to named instruments and agencies with realistic timelines. A DFI investment officer can cite the brief in an investment committee paper, because the blue finance section is bounded by its own data quality caveats, the carbon crediting readiness claim is calibrated to VM0033 requirements, and the fisheries value figures are accompanied by the sensitivity range and validation dependency that due diligence requires.
