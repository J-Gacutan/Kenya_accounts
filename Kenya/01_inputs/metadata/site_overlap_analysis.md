## Overlap Categories

### Triple overlap — coral reef + seagrass + mangrove

Two sites only:

- **Ngomeni** — coral reef (CCVA fish, KLF, PU-GOAP), seagrass cover and health, mangrove plots, and fisheries landing site. Highest-priority site for integrated seascape accounting. Only site where all four data streams converge.
- **Marereni** — coral reef (CCVA inverts, KLF, PU-GOAP), seagrass cover and health, mangrove plots. No fisheries landing data.

### Double overlap — coral reef + seagrass

Nine sites: Wesa, Roka, Malindi MPA, Kuruwitu, Bofa, Takaungu, Kanamai/Jumba ruins, Marereni, Ngomeni.

This is the strongest pairing in the dataset. Covers the full length of the monitored Kilifi coastline from Kanamai in the south to Ngomeni/Malindi in the north. Seagrass and coral reef data collected in the same year (2024) at all nine sites.

### Double overlap — mangrove + fisheries

- **Ngomeni** — mangrove plots + KeFS landing site
- **Kilifi Creek** — mangrove plots + KeFS landing site (no reef or seagrass data)

### Mangrove isolation

Mida Creek and Kilifi Creek have mangrove data only. No coral reef or seagrass surveys conducted at these sites. Cannot be used for cross-ecosystem condition linkage with current data.

### Sites with repeated coral reef measurement

Wesa and Roka are the strongest coral reef time series sites: CCVA and/or PU-GOAP surveys in November 2024, followed by COMRED fish UVC and coral cover in July 2025. Both also have seagrass data from 2024. The KLF consolidated fish file (File 7) is pre-structured for annual updates through 2092, indicating the design intent for long-term monitoring at these sites.

Uyombo has two coral reef time points (KLF Nov 2024 + COMRED Jul 2025) but no seagrass or mangrove data.

## Recommendations for SEEA EA Accounts

### Priority integrated sites

1. **Ngomeni** — Use as the primary integrated seascape site. Only location with reef + seagrass + mangrove + fisheries data. Supports cross-ecosystem condition and provisioning service accounts.
2. **Wesa and Roka** — Use as primary condition time series sites. Two coral reef measurement points (Nov 2024 + Jul 2025) plus seagrass 2024. Best candidates for detecting change between SEEA EA accounting periods.
3. **Marereni** — Secondary integrated site (reef + seagrass + mangrove; no fisheries). Cross-ecosystem condition comparison possible.

### Accounting period alignment

The two coral reef time points (Nov 2024, Jul 2025) straddle the 2025 SEEA EA accounting period start. Treat Nov 2024 as the opening condition baseline and Jul 2025 as the first in-period measurement. Seagrass and mangrove data (both 2024-2025) align with the opening baseline.

Fisheries 2020-2024 provides a pre-accounting-period trend baseline; 2025 data (when KeFS Bulletin released) will be the first in-period provisioning service data point.

### Data gaps for integrated accounts

- Mangrove sites Mida Creek and Kilifi Creek have no reef or seagrass spatial counterparts; treat as extent-only inputs for now.
- Fisheries landings (KeFS) are county-aggregate; cannot be attributed to specific reef, seagrass, or mangrove patches without spatial disaggregation using additional effort or vessel monitoring data.
- Seagrass has no interannual time series; a repeat survey in 2026 would enable change detection aligned with the SEEA EA accounting period.
- Mangrove is a single snapshot (Dec 2025); no change data available until resurvey.
