# Search Plan: Finding Baseline Reference Levels for Condition Indicators
**Madagascar Coral Reef M1.3 Condition Account**

---

## Overview

This document provides a systematic search strategy to locate **baseline/reference values** for each ecosystem condition indicator from peer-reviewed literature, with regional focus on **Western Indian Ocean (WIO), Indian Ocean, and Indo-Pacific coral reef ecosystems**.

**Goal:** Validate and complement existing references; identify regional specificity where available.

---

## INDICATOR 1: FISH BIOMASS (kg/ha)

### Current Reference
- **MacNeil et al. 2015** (source_2026_wio_019): 500 kg/ha for **unfished Indo-Pacific coral reefs** (pristine baseline; n=42 sites)
- **Issue:** Global/pristine reference; may not reflect "healthy" state for fished reefs; no Madagascar-specific precedent

### What Counts as a Valid Baseline
✅ Peer-reviewed studies reporting fish biomass density (kg/ha) at coral reefs
✅ Studies with explicit reference condition (pristine, unfished, protected, or target state)
✅ Multi-site averages (n ≥ 3 sites) to reduce individual site variance
✅ Clear methodology (visual census, or conversion method disclosed)
✅ Regional context: WIO, Indian Ocean, Indo-Pacific, or comparable tropical reef

### Search Terms & Modifiers
**Primary searches (in Google Scholar, Web of Science, Scopus):**
```
("coral reef" OR "reef fish") AND ("biomass" OR "standing stock")
AND (kg/ha OR kg ha⁻¹ OR tonnes/ha)
AND ("Indian Ocean" OR "WIO" OR "Western Indian Ocean" OR "Indo-Pacific"
     OR "Madagascar" OR "Kenya" OR "Mozambique" OR "Tanzania" OR "Seychelles")
AND ("reference level" OR "baseline" OR "pristine" OR "unfished"
     OR "healthy reef" OR "target")
```

**Variant searches:**
- `("fish biomass" OR "standing crop") AND ("coral reef" OR "reef ecosystem") AND ("reference" OR "target state" OR "undamaged")`
- `("total reef fish biomass") AND ("Indian Ocean" OR "tropical") AND (year ≥ 2010)`
- `("biomass reference" OR "baseline biomass") AND ("reef fish") AND ("management" OR "conservation")`

**Taxonomic specificity:**
- If total biomass unavailable, search for **target commercial families** (Serranidae, Lutjanidae, Carangidae) separately and aggregate
- Search: `("reef fish biomass" OR "exploited species") AND ("family-level" OR "functional group") AND ("Indian Ocean")`

### Target Journals & Databases
| Source | Type | Coverage |
|--------|------|----------|
| **Coral Reefs** | Journal | Peer-reviewed; primary reef ecology venue; strong Indian Ocean coverage |
| **Marine Ecology Progress Series (MEPS)** | Journal | Quantitative ecology; biomass studies |
| **Fisheries Research** | Journal | Fish stock assessment methods; conversion factors |
| **Ecological Applications** | Journal | Restoration targets; reference condition |
| **Journal of Applied Ecology** | Journal | Conservation outcomes; PA effectiveness |
| **Web of Science** | Database | Cross-journal; advanced filtering by region, year, document type |
| **Google Scholar** | Database | Broader coverage; includes gray literature; good for WIO-specific studies |
| **ResearchGate** | Database | Author networks; pre-prints from Madagascar/Kenya researchers |

### Success Criteria
- ✅ **Found 3+ independent studies** with regional fish biomass references (WIO or Indo-Pacific)
- ✅ **Ranges documented:** e.g., "healthy reefs 400–600 kg/ha; degraded reefs 100–200 kg/ha"
- ✅ **Madagascar or East African specific reference** identified (even if from grey literature, e.g., MPRH reports)
- ✅ **Methodology clarity:** Study explicitly reports census method (UVC length, transect area, etc.) so comparability can be assessed

## INDICATOR 2: FISH SPECIES RICHNESS (species per site)

### Current Reference
- **Observed median:** 61 species per site (Madagascar 2025–26 data)
- **Historical baseline:** 85 species (from 1990s survey; lower taxonomy precision)
- **Issue:** No explicit "healthy reef" richness target; variation high across reefs

### What Counts as a Valid Baseline
✅ Peer-reviewed studies reporting species richness (species count) at coral reefs
✅ Explicit reference condition (pristine, undamaged, protected, or high-protection PA)
✅ Same or comparable sampling method (UVC at depth 2–20 m; transect length ≥15 m)
✅ Regional context: WIO, Indian Ocean, or Indo-Pacific
✅ Sample size clarity: n sites, effort per site

### Search Terms & Modifiers
**Primary searches:**
```
("coral reef" OR "reef fish") AND ("species richness" OR "species diversity" OR "fish diversity"
OR "species count" OR "species number")
AND ("reference" OR "baseline" OR "pristine" OR "unfished" OR "healthy" OR "undamaged")
AND ("Indian Ocean" OR "WIO" OR "Indo-Pacific" OR "Madagascar" OR "Kenya" OR "Seychelles")
AND ("visual census" OR "UVC" OR "underwater visual survey" OR year ≥ 2005)
```

**Variant searches:**
- `("fish diversity" OR "species richness") AND ("coral reef" OR "reef ecosystem") AND ("protected area" OR "marine reserve")`
- `("reef fish diversity") AND ("baseline" OR "reference condition") AND ("tropical" OR "Indo-Pacific")`
- `("species accumulation" OR "diversity gradient") AND ("coral reef") AND ("Indian Ocean" OR "Western Indian Ocean")`

**Taxonomic scope:**
- If total richness unavailable, search by functional group: `("herbivorous fish diversity" OR "corallivore richness") AND ("reef")`

### Target Journals & Databases
| Source | Rationale |
|--------|-----------|

| **Journal of Biogeography** | Regional patterns; WIO vs. Indo-Pacific comparisons |
| **Web of Science / Scopus** | Filter: (coral reef OR reef fish) AND (richness OR diversity) AND region |

- ✅ **Found 3+ regional studies** with richness data and reference conditions
- ✅ **Richness range identified:** e.g., "pristine reefs 80–120 spp.; degraded reefs 30–60 spp."
- ✅ **Madagascar or East African data** (even observational; e.g., Randriarimanantena et al. 2003 historical survey)

## INDICATOR 3: TROPHIC FUNCTIONAL GROUP COMPOSITION (% biomass)

### Current Reference
- **Target composition (from Ecopath models):** Herbivores 40–50%, Corallivores 5–10%, Piscivores 25–35%
- **Observed composition (Madagascar 2025–26):** Herbivores 40.6%, Carnivores 30.0%, Planktivores 15.2%, Corallivores 8.7%, others <5%
- **Issue:** Target is model-based (Palomares & Pauly 2009); no empirical WIO baseline documented

### What Counts as a Valid Baseline
✅ Peer-reviewed studies reporting trophic/functional group composition at coral reefs
✅ Explicit categorization: herbivore, piscivore, planktivore, corallivore, omnivore
✅ Reported as % biomass (not % count, which inflates small fish)
✅ Reference condition: pristine, undamaged, highly protected, or "healthy reef"
✅ Regional: WIO, Indian Ocean, Indo-Pacific coral reefs

### Search Terms & Modifiers
**Primary searches:**
```
("coral reef" OR "reef fish") AND ("trophic" OR "functional group" OR "guild composition"
OR "feeding group")
AND ("herbivore" OR "piscivore" OR "planktivore" OR "corallivore" OR "functional diversity")
AND ("% biomass" OR "biomass proportion" OR "trophic structure")
AND ("baseline" OR "reference" OR "pristine" OR "healthy" OR "unfished")
AND ("Indian Ocean" OR "WIO" OR "Indo-Pacific" OR "Madagascar")
```

**Variant searches:**
- `("trophic structure" OR "food web") AND ("coral reef") AND ("reference condition" OR "healthy state")`
- `("herbivore biomass" OR "piscivore biomass") AND ("coral reef") AND ("% of total" OR "proportion")`
- `("functional group biomass") AND ("reef fish") AND ("protected area" OR "marine reserve") AND ("Indian Ocean")`

**Model-based reference searches:**
- `(Palomares OR Pauly) AND ("Ecopath" OR "ecosystem model") AND ("coral reef" OR "reef fish") AND (trophic OR guild)`

### Target Journals & Databases
| Source | Rationale |
|--------|-----------|
| **Coral Reefs** | Primary source for trophic structure studies |
| **Marine Ecology Progress Series** | Quantitative trophic analysis |
| **Ecological Monographs** | Detailed ecosystem structure |

| **Ecosystems** | Functional group dynamics |
| **Web of Science** | Advanced search: (trophic OR functional) AND (reef) AND (biomass) AND (structure) |

### Success Criteria

## INDICATOR 4: CROWN-OF-THORNS (COTS) DENSITY (ind/ha)

### Current Reference
- **Outbreak threshold:** 30 ind/ha (convention; outbreak causing coral mortality)
- **Madagascar 2025–26 observed:** 7.4 ind/ha (low; from only 3 of 27 sites)
- **Issue:** Very few observations; scaling small transect counts to /ha inflates variance; no WIO regional baseline established

### What Counts as a Valid Baseline
✅ Peer-reviewed studies reporting COTS abundance/density (ind/ha or ind/100m²)
✅ Explicit reference condition: background density, outbreak threshold, or recovery baseline
✅ Regional context: WIO, Indian Ocean, or Indo-Pacific (COTS are Indo-Pacific endemic)
✅ Clear sampling method: timed search, transect, or quadrat with area disclosed
✅ Temporal context: if outbreak data, note the year

### Search Terms & Modifiers
**Primary searches:**
```
("Acanthaster" OR "COTS" OR "crown-of-thorns" OR "starfish outbreak")
AND ("density" OR "abundance" OR "ind/ha" OR "count per hectare" OR "population")
AND ("baseline" OR "reference" OR "background level" OR "pre-outbreak")
AND ("coral reef" OR "reef ecosystem")
AND ("Indian Ocean" OR "WIO" OR "Indo-Pacific" OR "Great Barrier Reef" OR "Madagascar")
```

**Variant searches:**
- `("Acanthaster planci") AND ("density" OR "distribution") AND ("reef" OR "coral")`
- `("crown-of-thorns outbreak") AND ("baseline" OR "background" OR "normal density")`
- `("COTS control" OR "COTS removal") AND ("density before" OR "initial density")`

**Outbreak history:**
- `("Acanthaster outbreak") AND ("Indian Ocean" OR "WIO") AND (year 2015–2023) AND ("density" OR "abundance")`

### Target Journals & Databases

| **Google Scholar** | Gray literature on COTS control (often gov't reports with density data) |
| **ResearchGate** | Contact WIO reef researchers (e.g., Tanzania, Kenya, Mozambique) for unpublished COTS surveys |

### Success Criteria

## CRITICAL APPRAISAL CHECKLIST

When evaluating a source for inclusion as a "baseline":

- [ ] **Peer-reviewed or credible institution?** (≥ MEDIUM confidence)
- [ ] **Methods transparent?** (Can I assess comparability to Madagascar UVC?)
- [ ] **Sample size adequate?** (n ≥ 3 sites for multi-site averages; or clear single-site context)
- [ ] **Reference condition explicit?** (Does the paper state "pristine," "healthy," "unfished," or "protected"?)
- [ ] **Regional relevance?** (WIO, Indian Ocean, Indo-Pacific, or clearly analogous)
- [ ] **Temporal specificity?** (When was data collected? Recent <10 yrs preferred)
- [ ] **Quantitative values reported?** (Actual numbers, not qualitative descriptions)
- [ ] **Uncertainty ranges?** (SD, SE, CI, or range provided?)

## EXECUTION ROADMAP

### Phase 1: Quick Scan (1 week)
1. **Google Scholar** quick search for each indicator (5–10 min per indicator)
2. **Identify 5–10 promising papers** per indicator
3. **Extract title, authors, year, region, key values**

### Phase 2: Detailed Review (2 weeks)
1. **Access full-text papers** (through institutional library, ResearchGate, or contact authors)
2. **Document reference values** in standardised table (see outputs below)
3. **Assess comparability** to Madagascar methods
4. **Flag data gaps** (e.g., "no WIO data found for COTS baseline")

### Phase 3: Regional Outreach (1 week)
1. **Email WIO researchers** (ResearchGate or institutional contacts) asking about unpublished baselines
2. **Query MPRH, KMFRI, University of Dar es Salaam** for grey literature (government reports, baseline surveys)
3. **Check SPC, FAO, UNEP reports** for regional ecosystem baselines

### Phase 4: Synthesis (1 week)
1. **Consolidate findings** in standardised reference table
2. **Identify gaps** (e.g., "no WIO sea urchin baseline found; recommend Indo-Pacific global reference")
3. **Write short methods note:** "Reference level validation and regional comparison" section for publication
