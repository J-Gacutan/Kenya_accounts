## Indicator 4: Crown-of-Thorns Starfish (COTS) Density (ind/ha)

| Reference | Region | COTS Density (ind/ha) | Category | Confidence |
|-----------|--------|----------------------|----------|------------|
| General Indo-Pacific literature (Birkeland & Lucas 1990; AIMS) | Indo-Pacific | Background: ~1–10 ind/ha; no detectable coral loss | Background / healthy baseline | HIGH |
| Outbreak definition (multiple sources) | Indo-Pacific | Outbreak: >10–15 ind/ha (lower bound); severe: 140–1,000+ ind/ha | Outbreak threshold | HIGH |
| Dulvy et al. (2021). *Nature Communications* | Indo-Pacific | Fish predators suppress COTS to background <1–2 ind/ha | Background (predator-controlled) | HIGH |
| Sheppard et al. (2015). *Marine Biology* 162 | Central Indian Ocean (Chagos) | Localised outbreak zones: high densities; background elsewhere <1/ha | Outbreak / background contrast | HIGH |
| Madagascar 2025–26 observed | SW Madagascar | 7.4 ind/ha (from 3 of 27 sites) | Current (low) | HIGH |

**Recommended reference for SEEA EA account:**
- Background / healthy reef: **<1 ind/ha** (pristine/predator-controlled)
- Threshold for concern: **10–15 ind/ha**
- Outbreak: **>15–30 ind/ha**
- Current Madagascar value (7.4 ind/ha from 3 sites) falls in elevated-background range; not outbreak
- Key linkage: piscivore biomass (Indicator 1/3) predicts COTS suppression (Dulvy et al. 2021)

**Indicator confidence: HIGH** — well-established Indo-Pacific thresholds; WIO values align with global

## Publication Methods Note (Draft)

> "We validated reference levels by consulting 18+ peer-reviewed sources from the Western Indian Ocean (WIO) and Indo-Pacific regions (Phase 1 systematic search, March 2026). Fish biomass reference was updated from MacNeil et al. (2015) to the WIO-specific conservation target of 1,150 kg/ha and sustainability floor of 600 kg/ha (McClanahan et al. 2016, 2020), more appropriate for managed reef contexts. Fish species richness was benchmarked against the WIO per-site range of 82–152 species (Samoilys et al. 2019; Gudka et al. 2022). Trophic functional group composition was referenced against global averages (herbivores + invertivores ~56% of standing biomass; piscivores ~13%; Hicks et al. 2023) in the absence of WIO-specific empirical benchmarks. COTS density thresholds were confirmed against Indo-Pacific literature (background <1 ind/ha; outbreak >15 ind/ha; Dulvy et al. 2021). Critical data gaps remain for sea urchin density (no WIO reference condition formally published) and Tridacna clam abundance (no SW Madagascar baseline). These indicators are presented with explicit uncertainty (LOW confidence), and provisional values are drawn from the nearest regional analogues (Kenya: 1.5–2 ind/m² for Echinometra mathaei; Lakshadweep: 122–141 ind/ha for T. maxima). Future WIO regional monitoring and consultation with GCRMN East Africa and MPRH are recommended."

## Phase 1 Gap Analysis

### Gaps Closed
- Fish biomass: WIO-specific peer-reviewed references found (McClanahan 2016, 2020)
- COTS: Background and outbreak thresholds well-established; Indo-Pacific consensus confirmed
- Fish richness: WIO per-site range documented (Samoilys 2019; Gudka 2022); SW Madagascar (Chabanet 2005) found

### Gaps Partially Closed
- Trophic composition: Global averages available; WIO-specific empirical benchmarks not found
- Clam abundance: Indian Ocean reference (Lakshadweep) found; no WIO/Madagascar baseline

### Critical Gaps Remaining
- Sea urchin: No formal WIO reference condition published; provisional values only
- Clam abundance: No SW Madagascar data; WIO populations severely depleted
