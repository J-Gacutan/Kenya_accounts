## Context: Why This Matters for M1.3 Reef Condition Accounts

Madagascar's coral reef condition accounting (M1.3 Photic Coral Reefs) operates within a **rapidly evolving policy environment**. The 2024–2025 policy restructuring directly affects:

| SEEA Component | Policy Influence | Data Impact | 2026 Status |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Extent (Reef Area)** | SNEB Axe 3 (Marine Spatial Planning); BBNJ high-seas nominations | MPA boundaries; habitat mapping | Sentinel-2 mapping in progress (Mar 2026) |
| **Condition (Ecosystem Health)** | Decree 2006-400 revised (megafauna monitoring); OCPP seagrass protocol | Population monitoring; acidification baseline | Standardized protocols being finalized |
| **Services (Livelihood Value)** | ILO Just Transition; alternative livelihood programs; SNEB Axe 2 | Landing site data; fisher income; market prices | Landing site collection needs enforcement |

## The Five Strategic Axes of SNEB & Ecosystem Accounting

### How SNEB Aligns with SEEA EA Pillars

```
SNEB Axe 1 (Governance)
  ↓ Supports ↓
SEEA EXTENT (spatial definition via Marine Spatial Planning)

SNEB Axe 2 (Natural Resource Valuation)
  ↓ Supports ↓
SEEA SERVICES (resource pricing; market value assessment)

SNEB Axe 3 (Infrastructure & Services)
  ↓ Supports ↓
SEEA EXTENT (infrastructure planning affects spatial scope)
+ SEEA CONDITION (monitoring infrastructure)

SNEB Axe 4 (Water & Energy)
  ↓ Supports (indirectly) ↓
SEEA CONDITION (energy alternatives reduce fishing pressure)

SNEB Axe 5 (Climate Resilience)
  ↓ Supports ↓
SEEA CONDITION (blue carbon projects indicate ecosystem health)
+ SEEA VALUATION (climate service value)
```

### Operational Implication

The SNEB's five-axis structure means that **reef condition accounts should reference these frameworks** when justifying monitoring methodologies and service valuations:

- **Fish biomass** (condition) → Supported by Axe 2 resource valuation mandates
- **Invertebrate indicators** (condition) → Supported by Axe 1 governance transparency requirements
- **Fisheries landings** (services) → Supported by Decree 2016-1492 (updated via Axe 2)
- **Fisher income** (services) → Supported by ILO Just Transition framework

## BBNJ Entry into Force: Impact on Reef Accounting Scope

### Key Date: January 17, 2026

**What Changed:** Madagascar now has legal mandate to participate in high-seas governance adjacent to its EEZ.

**Accounting Implication:**

| Reef Type | Pre-Jan 17, 2026 | Post-Jan 17, 2026 | New Consideration |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Coastal Reefs (0–12 nm)** | Full national jurisdiction | Unchanged | No change |
| **EEZ Reefs (12 nm–200 nm)** | Madagascar jurisdiction | Unchanged | No change |
| **Adjacent High Seas** | De facto no governance | Madagascar can nominate MPAs | Potential scope expansion for "adjacent ecosystem" valuation |

**Practical Guidance:**

For M1.3 reef condition accounting in 2026–2030:
1. **Primary focus** remains coastal/EEZ reefs (national jurisdiction)
2. **Secondary consideration:** High-seas areas adjacent to Madagascar coast (BBNJ CoP1 2026 will clarify MPA nominations)
3. **Monitoring implications:** BBNJ-related high-seas activities may affect adjacent reef condition (pollution, climate impacts); track for risk assessment

## Landing Site Data Collection: Critical Gap for Services Accounting

### The Mandate (Decree 2016-1492 — Still Active)

**Requirement:** All landing sites must report catch by species, weight, and fisher identification.

**Current Status (2026):**
- Mandate exists in law
- Enforcement inconsistent across regions
- Data collection not systematic

**Impact on Services Accounting:**

Services accounting requires:
1. **Fisheries Landings** → Catch data by species (in kg/year)
2. **Fisher Income** → Income distribution by fisher household
3. **Market Prices** → Price per kg by species (MISSING)

**Actionable 2026 Priority:**

```
Immediate:
1. Request MPEB to enforce Decree 2016-1492 landing site data collection at 10 pilot ports
2. Establish baseline of current landing volumes by species (estimate from existing observer data)
3. Survey market prices at 5 regional fish markets (SW Madagascar priority)

Medium-term (2027):
1. Scale up landing site monitoring to all major ports
2. Integrate data into fisheries landings databases (accessible to SEEA team)
3. Link to fisher income surveys (ILO Just Transition framework data)

Long-term (2028–2030):
1. Systematic landing data feed into ecosystem accounting dashboards
2. Annual updates to services valuations
3. Scenario analysis on alternative livelihood impacts
```

## Marine Megafauna Protection Update: Implication for Condition Accounts

### Decree 2006-400 Revision (Completed Late 2025)

**What Changed:**
- Old: 5 sea turtle species protected
- New: 94 species protected (whales, dolphins, dugongs, sharks, rays, coelacanths, threatened fish)

**Condition Account Implication:**

The expanded protection list enables **by-catch monitoring** in fisheries condition assessments:

| Species Group | Monitoring Benefit | Data Source |
|---|---|---|
| **Sea Turtles** | Legal basis for by-catch restrictions; enforcement | Landing site observers; MPA patrols |
| **Sharks/Rays** | Can now track threatened elasmobranch populations | Catch data disaggregation needed |
| **Marine Mammals** | Whale/dolphin by-catch monitoring | Observer programs; acoustic monitoring |

**Operational Requirement (2026):**

Request OCPP (Ocean Country Partnership Programme) Phase 2 support for:
1. Landing site observer training on megafauna identification
2. By-catch data standardization protocols
3. Integration into reef condition assessment framework

## Alternative Livelihoods & Condition Improvement Feedback Loop

### Policy Support for Livelihood Transition

**SNEB Axis 2 + ILO Just Transition Framework supports:**
- Mangrove apiculture (reduces timber cutting pressure on mangrove condition)
- Sea cucumber farming (reduces harvest of wild populations)
- Eco-tourism (increases value of healthy reefs → conservation incentive)

### Condition Accounting Benefit

As alternative livelihoods reduce fishing pressure → **reef condition improves**.

**2026–2030 Scenario Analysis Opportunity:**

```
Scenario A (Status Quo):
- Alternative livelihoods reach 10% of fishing households
- Fishing pressure remains high
- Reef condition: Stable/slight decline

Scenario B (Accelerated Transition):
- Alternative livelihoods reach 30% of fishing households (via ILO/UNEP support)
- Fishing pressure declines 20%
- Reef condition: Improvement pathway to "Good" condition

Scenario C (Conservation+Transition):
- Alternative livelihoods reach 50% + MPA network expands to 30% coverage
- Fishing pressure declines 50%
- Reef condition: Recovery toward "Very Good" condition
```

**Analytical Framework:** Reference SNEB Axe 2 (Natural Resource Valuation) when justifying livelihood-condition linkages in ecosystem accounts.

## Blue Carbon Projects & Ecosystem Valuation

### Tahiry Honko & Ma Honkô Significance

**Direct Relevance to Reef Accounts:**

Mangroves provide multiple services connected to reef ecosystems:
- **Coastal protection** (buffers reefs from storm surge)
- **Nursery habitat** (fish larvae recruit from mangroves to reefs)
- **Water filtration** (reduces silt/nutrient stress on reefs)
- **Carbon storage** (climate mitigation = reef resilience)

### Mangrove Carbon Valuation Model (Replicable for Reef Services)

**Tahiry Honko Method:**
1. Measure mangrove area (1,200+ ha)
2. Calculate carbon sequestration rate (Plan Vivo methodology)
3. Monetize via voluntary carbon market (Plan Vivo-verified credits)
4. Distribute benefits to local communities

**Applicable to Reefs:**
- Reef tourism value (similar to mangrove honey value)
- Reef fisheries nursery value (fish recruitment to offshore reefs)
- Reef climate resilience value (wave attenuation; storm protection)

**2026 Action:** Develop reef ecosystem service valuation methodology informed by Tahiry Honko mangrove carbon model.

## ILO Just Transition & Labor-Based Data Collection

### Framework Relevance to Services Accounting

**ILO Tools Available for Madagascar:**
- Supply and Use Tables (SUT) — Estimate ecosystem services labor inputs
- Green Jobs Assessment Models (GJAM) — Forecast livelihood impacts

**How This Supports Reef Services:**

```

  Labor input (fisher hours) → SUT
  + Output value (catch kg × price) → SUT
  = Total value added to economy

+ Job impacts from marine conservation policies → GJAM
  (e.g., impact of MPA enforcement on fisher employment)

= Holistic services assessment integrating labor equity
```

## Ocean Acidification & Reef Condition Baseline

### Nairobi Convention ROAAP (Regional Ocean Acidification Action Plan)

**Status:** Adopted 2022; Madagascar committed to monitoring

**Reef Condition Implication:**

Ocean acidification is a **threat to condition account baselines**:
- Coral calcification rates decline with lower pH
- Mollusk shells become thinner; oyster recruitment impaired
- Fish otolith development affected

### Operational Priority (2026–2027)

```
Step 1: Establish acidification baseline
  - pH monitoring at 3–5 reef sites (SW Madagascar priority)
  - Monthly sampling; one-year baseline (2026)

Step 2: Link acidification to condition metrics
  - Measure any pH-condition correlation
  - Assess vulnerability of key indicator species to acidification

Step 3: Incorporate into risk assessment
  - 2030 condition trajectory accounting for acidification stress
  - Scenario analysis: "condition if acidification continues vs. if reversed"

Step 4: Policy advocacy
  - Use ROAAP monitoring data to influence climate action priorities
```

**Document to Reference:** See commitment_008 in policy_rag_sample_data.json (ROAAP) and linkage_008 (ICZM Protocol ocean acidification component).

## Conclusion

Madagascar's 2024–2030 policy restructuring creates **unprecedented opportunities** for ecosystem accounting integration. The shift toward Blue Economy thinking means:

- **Extent accounts** are now embedded in Marine Spatial Planning frameworks
- **Condition accounts** can reference mandatory monitoring protocols (OCPP, ROAAP, megafauna protection)
- **Services accounts** have policy-backed landing site data mandates and alternative livelihood transition pathways

**Implementation remains the critical bottleneck** — policies exist, but enforcement and data systematization are inconsistent.

**The 2026–2030 period is a "window of opportunity" to institutionalize reef ecosystem accounting within the SNEB framework, ensuring that reef condition becomes a central metric for Blue Economy success.**
